# Bayesian Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM)

## Project Overview

This project implements a **Bayesian Marketing Mix Model (MMM)** to quantify the causal impact of paid media channels on sales and evaluate their efficiency.

Marketing Mix Modeling is a statistical technique used to decompose total sales into:

- **Base sales**: driven by organic demand, seasonality, and external factors
- **Incremental sales**: generated by marketing activities

This project follows a modern MMM framework inspired by Google's Bayesian methodology, incorporating **carryover effects (Adstock)** and **diminishing returns (Saturation)**, and estimating channel contributions using a **Bayesian regression model implemented in PyMC**.

The objective is to understand media effectiveness and generate insights to support **data-driven budget allocation decisions**.

---

## Dataset

A simulated weekly dataset was used, containing:

- TV spend
- YouTube spend
- Facebook spend
- Search spend
- Total sales

Weekly granularity was chosen to reflect standard industry practice, as recommended in the MMM literature.

The simulated structure allows full control over the modeling pipeline while preserving realistic media spend dynamics.

---

## Methodology

The modeling process follows the standard MMM workflow:

### 1. Adstock Transformation

Advertising effects persist over time due to delayed consumer response. This carryover effect is modeled using the Adstock function:

\[
Adstock_t = Spend_t + \theta \cdot Adstock_{t-1}
\]

Where:

- θ represents the carryover strength
- Higher θ implies longer-lasting effects

---

### 2. Saturation Transformation

Marketing response exhibits diminishing returns at higher spend levels. This effect is modeled using an exponential saturation function:

\[
Saturation = 1 - exp(-\lambda \cdot Adstock)
\]

Where:

- λ controls the rate of diminishing returns
- Higher λ results in faster saturation

---

### 3. Bayesian Regression Model

A Bayesian linear regression was implemented using PyMC, where:

- Target variable: weekly sales
- Predictors: transformed media variables (Adstock + Saturation)

The model estimates:

- Media contribution per channel
- Base sales component
- Posterior distributions for all parameters

The Bayesian framework allows robust estimation and uncertainty quantification.

## Key Results

### Sales decomposition

The model decomposed total sales into:

- **Base sales: 62.7%**
- **Media-driven sales: 37.3%**

This indicates that marketing activities contribute significantly to overall performance.

---

### Channel efficiency (ROAS)

Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) was estimated for each channel:

| Channel | ROAS |
|--------|------|
| YouTube | 1.09 |
| Facebook | 0.90 |
| Search | 0.28 |
| TV | 0.06 |

YouTube was identified as the most efficient channel, while TV showed the lowest efficiency.

---

### Response curves and saturation

Response curves were generated to analyze diminishing returns and saturation behavior.

Key observations:

- YouTube reaches maximum contribution at relatively low spend levels, indicating high efficiency
- TV requires significantly higher investment to achieve similar contribution levels
- Digital channels saturate faster but deliver higher marginal returns
- TV shows slow saturation but low marginal efficiency

These curves provide a quantitative basis for budget optimization.

## Conclusions

This project demonstrates how Bayesian Marketing Mix Modeling can be used to quantify marketing effectiveness and support strategic decision-making.

The main conclusions are:

- Marketing contributes substantially to total sales, accounting for over one-third of revenue
- Significant differences in efficiency exist between channels
- YouTube shows the strongest performance, generating high contribution with relatively low investment
- TV exhibits low efficiency and requires large budgets to generate incremental impact
- Response curves confirm the presence of diminishing returns across all channels

From a strategic perspective, these results suggest that reallocating budget toward more efficient digital channels could improve overall marketing performance.
